Date: 2024-05-20
Description: This Statutory Rule is made under section 2(1) of the Period Products (Free Provision) Act (Northern Ireland) 2022. It specifies the public service bodies that are subject to the duty in section 2(3) of the Act (specified public service bodies). It specifies which premises are to be treated as premises of a specified public service body for the purposes of the Act, describes when these premises are considered to be in use for the purposes of the Act and provides for descriptions of persons to be treated as in those specified public service bodies’ premises for the purposes of the Act.

## The Period Products (the Department for the Economy Specified Public Service Bodies) Regulations (Northern Ireland) 2024

    Period Products (free Provision)

Laid before the Assembly in draft

  Made   29th April 2024

  Coming into operation    29th April 2024

      The Department for the Economy  makes the following Regulations in
exercise of the powers conferred by section 2(1), (2)(c) and (d), (7)(a), (b)
and (c), and (9) of the Period Products (Free Provision) Act (Northern
Ireland) 2022  and after consultation in accordance with section 2(8) of that
Act.

### Citation and commencement

1

These Regulations may be cited as the Period Products (the Department for the
Economy Specified Public Service Bodies) Regulations (Northern Ireland) 2024
and shall come into operation on 29th April 2024.

### Interpretation

2

2.1

In these Regulations—

      “ the Department ” means the Department for the Economy;           “ in
use ” is to be construed in accordance with regulation 4(b);           “
learning ” means the acquisition of knowledge or skills through study,
experience, or being taught;           “ non-educational purposes ” means
activities relating to administrative matters, student accommodation,
canteen/eating facilities, leisure facilities and sport facilities;
“ premises ” is to be construed in accordance with regulation 4(a);
“ staff ” means employees, agency workers or volunteers in the premises of a
specified public service body;           “ students ” means people enrolled
with the relevant specified public service body;           “ the Act ” means
the Period Products (Free Provision) Act (Northern Ireland) 2022; and
“ visitors ” means a person with legitimate reason for being in the premises
of a specified public service body, such as attending meetings or organised
events.

### Descriptions of persons and premises

4

For the purposes of section 2(3) of the Act—

4.a

the descriptions of premises listed in column 2 of the Table below are to be
treated as being premises of the specified public service body listed at
column 1; and

4.b

the premises listed in column 2 of the Table below will be considered to be in
use when they are open for student and/or staff use; and

4.c

the descriptions of persons listed at column 3 of the Table below are to be
treated as being in the premises of the specified public service body listed
at column 1.

  Table       Column 1 - Specified Public Service Body   Column 2 -
Descriptions of Premises   Column 3- Descriptions of persons in premises
(a) (i) – (vi) Further Education Colleges All buildings, both those used for
learning and those used for non-educational purposes, which form part of the
premises of the body.     (i) students;       (ii) staff; and       (iii)
visitors     (b) (i)-(iv) Higher Education Institutions All buildings, both
those used for learning and those used for non-educational purposes, which
form part of the premises of the body.     (i) students;       (ii) staff; and
(iii) visitors.

## EXPLANATORY NOTE

    (This note is not part of the Regulations)

  This Statutory Rule is made under section 2(1) of the Period Products (Free
Provision) Act (Northern Ireland) 2022. It specifies the public service bodies
that are subject to the duty in section 2(3) of the Act (specified public
service bodies). It specifies which premises are to be treated as premises of
a specified public service body for the purposes of the Act, describes when
these premises are considered to be in use for the purposes of the Act and
provides for descriptions of persons to be treated as in those specified
public service bodies’ premises for the purposes of the Act.
