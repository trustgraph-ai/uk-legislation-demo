## The Road Traffic (Fixed Penalty) (Offences) (Amendment) Order (Northern Ireland) 2023

    Road Traffic And Vehicles

  Made   20th April 2023

  Coming into operation   15th May 2023

      The Department for Infrastructure , makes the following Order in
exercise of the powers conferred by Article 57(2) of the Road Traffic
Offenders (Northern Ireland) Order 1996  and now vested in it .           In
accordance with Article 91(1) of that Order the Department has consulted with
such representative organisations as it thinks fit.

### Citation and commencement

1

This Order may be cited as the Road Traffic (Fixed Penalty) (Offences)
(Amendment) Order (Northern Ireland) 2023 and shall come into operation on
15th May 2023.

### Amendment of the Road Traffic (Fixed Penalty) (Offences) Order

2

In Schedule 1 to the Road Traffic (Fixed Penalty) (Offences) Order (Northern
Ireland) 1997  (fixed penalty offences), after the heading “Offences under the
Road Traffic (Northern Ireland) Order 1995” and before item 20 insert—

      19A Article 12 Driving mechanically propelled vehicle on a road or other
public place without due care and attention, or without reasonable
consideration.

.

      Sealed with the Official Seal of the Department for Infrastructure on
20th April 2023         Chris Hughes   A senior officer of the   Department
for Infrastructure

## EXPLANATORY NOTE

    (This note is not part of the Order)

  This Order amends Schedule 1 to the Road Traffic (Fixed Penalty) (Offences)
Order (Northern Ireland) 1997 to specify an additional offence, relating to
the driving of a mechanically propelled vehicle on a road or other public
place without due care and attention, or without reasonable consideration for
other persons using the road or place, to be a fixed penalty offence for the
purposes of the Road Traffic Offenders (Northern Ireland) Order 1996.

  An Explanatory Memorandum has been produced and is available from Safe and
Accessible Travel Division, Department for Infrastructure, Clarence Court,
10-18 Adelaide Street, Town Parks, Belfast BT2 8GB or online alongside this
Statutory Rule at  http://www.legislation.gov.uk/nisr .
