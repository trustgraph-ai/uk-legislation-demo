## Offenders (Day of Release from Detention) Act 2023

      B e it enacted  by the King’s most Excellent Majesty, by and with the
advice and consent of the Lords Spiritual and Temporal, and Commons, in this
present Parliament assembled, and by the authority of the same, as follows:—

### Days on which offenders may be released from detention

1

1.1

Section 23 of the Criminal Justice Act 1961 (Prison Rules) is amended as
follows.

1.2

In subsection (3), after “shall” insert  “(subject to subsection (3B))” .

1.3

Before subsection (4) insert—

1.3.3B

The Secretary of State may direct that a prisoner who would, apart from this
subsection, be discharged on a working day immediately before a non-working
day, must instead be discharged on a day referred to in subsection  (3C) .

1.3.3C

The days are—

1.3.3C.a

the last eligible working day before the day on which the prisoner would
otherwise be discharged;

1.3.3C.b

the last eligible working day before that eligible working day.

1.3.3D

In this section—

      “ eligible working day ” means a working day that is not immediately
followed by a non-working day;           “ non-working day ” means—         a
Saturday or Sunday, Christmas Day, or Good Friday, or           any day that
is a bank holiday under section 1 of the Banking and Financial Dealings Act
1971 in England and Wales;                 “ working day ” means a day that is
not a non-working day.

1.4

After subsection (4) insert—

1.4.5

In subsections (3),  (3B)  and  (3C) , the references to a prisoner also
include references to a person detained in—

1.4.5.a

a secure children’s home, or

1.4.5.b

a secure 16 to 19 Academy,

in pursuance of a sentence or order referred to in section 163(1) of the
Police, Crime, Sentencing and Courts Act 2022.

1.4.6

In subsection  (5) —

      “ secure children’s home ” means—         a children’s home in England,
within the meaning given in section 1 of the Care Standards Act 2000, which
provides accommodation for the purposes of restricting liberty;
residential premises in Wales which provide a secure accommodation service,
within the meaning given in Part 1 of the  Regulation and Inspection of Social
Care (Wales) Act 2016 (anaw 2) ;                 “ secure 16 to 19 Academy ”
has the meaning given in section 1B of the Academies Act 2010.

### Offenders detained under International Criminal Court Act 2001

2

In paragraph 2(1)(c) of Schedule 7 to the International Criminal Court Act
2001 (domestic provisions not applicable to persons detained pursuant to a
sentence of the International Criminal Court)—

2.a

after “section 23(3)” insert  “and  (3B) ” ;

2.b

for “or on a holiday” substitute  “, on a holiday or on a working day
immediately before a non-working day” .

### Extent, commencement and short title

3

3.1

Section  1  extends to England and Wales.

3.2

Section  2  and this section extend to England and Wales, Scotland and
Northern Ireland.

3.3

This Act comes into force on such day as the Secretary of State may by
regulations appoint.

3.4

This Act may be cited as the Offenders (Day of Release from Detention) Act
2023.
