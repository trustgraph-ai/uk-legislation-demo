## Animals (Low-Welfare Activities Abroad) Act 2023

                                                   B e it enacted  by the
King’s most Excellent Majesty, by and with the advice and consent of the Lords
Spiritual and Temporal, and Commons, in this present Parliament assembled, and
by the authority of the same, as follows:—

### Low-welfare animal activities

#### Prohibition on sale of low-welfare animal activities

1

1.1

A person commits an offence if, in a relevant part of the United Kingdom, the
person sells, or offers or arranges to sell, any right to observe or
participate in an activity which—

1.1.a

involves an animal,

1.1.b

takes place outside the United Kingdom, and

1.1.c

is of a description specified for that part of the United Kingdom by
regulations made by the appropriate national authority (“ activity regulations
”).

1.2

The appropriate national authority may specify a description of activity in
activity regulations only if it considers that an activity of that description
involves or is likely to involve—

1.2.a

keeping the animal in conditions within  subsection (3) , or

1.2.b

subjecting it to treatment (whether before, during or after the activity)
within that subsection.

1.3

An animal is kept in conditions, or is subject to treatment, within this
subsection if keeping the animal in the conditions, or subjecting it to the
treatment, in the appropriate national authority’s part of the United Kingdom
would constitute an offence under the appropriate national legislation.

#### Prohibition on advertising of low-welfare animal activities

2

2.1

For the purposes of this section an advertisement is a prohibited
advertisement in a relevant part of the United Kingdom if the advertisement
has the purpose or effect of promoting the observation of, or participation
in, an activity which—

2.1.a

involves an animal,

2.1.b

takes place outside the United Kingdom, and

2.1.c

is of a description specified for that part of the United Kingdom by activity
regulations.

2.2

A person commits an offence if, in the course of a business, the person—

2.2.a

publishes in a relevant part of the United Kingdom an advertisement that is a
prohibited advertisement in that part, or

2.2.b

causes such an advertisement to be so published.

2.3

A person commits an offence if, in the course of a business, the person—

2.3.a

prints in a relevant part of the United Kingdom an advertisement published in
that part of the United Kingdom that is a prohibited advertisement in that
part, or

2.3.b

causes such an advertisement to be so printed.

2.4

A person commits an offence if, in the course of a business, the person—

2.4.a

distributes in a relevant part of the United Kingdom an advertisement
published in that part of the United Kingdom that is a prohibited
advertisement in that part, or

2.4.b

causes such an advertisement to be so distributed.

2.5

A person does not commit an offence under  subsection (2)  or  (4)  if the
advertisement is contained in a publication (other than an in-flight
magazine)—

2.5.a

which is printed outside a relevant part of the United Kingdom, and

2.5.b

whose principal market is not, or does not include, a relevant part of the
United Kingdom or any part of one.

2.6

A person does not commit an offence under  subsection (4)  if—

2.6.a

the distribution is by means of electronic transmission, and

2.6.b

the person did not carry on business in a relevant part of the United Kingdom
at the time of the distribution.

2.7

A person does not commit an offence under  subsection (4)  by selling a
publication to a member of the public.

2.8

It is a defence for a person charged with an offence under  subsection (3)  or
(4)  in relation to the printing or distribution of an advertisement in a
relevant part of the United Kingdom to prove that the person did not know, and
had no reason to suspect, that the advertisement would be published in that
part.

### Enforcement

#### Offences: penalties, prosecution and liability

3

3.1

A person who commits an offence under this Act is liable—

3.1.a

on summary conviction in England and Wales, to a fine;

3.1.b

on summary conviction in Northern Ireland, to a fine not exceeding level 5 on
the standard scale.

3.2

The following do not apply in relation to offences under this Act—

3.2.a

section 127 of the Magistrates’ Courts Act 1980 (limitation of time);

3.2.b

article 19 of the Magistrates’ Courts (Northern Ireland) Order 1981 ( S.I.
1981/1675 (N.I. 26) ) (time limit for making complaint).

3.3

Where an offence under this Act committed by a body corporate—

3.3.a

is committed with the consent or connivance of any director, manager,
secretary or other similar officer of the body corporate, or any person who
was purporting to act in any such capacity, or

3.3.b

is attributable to any neglect on the part of any such person,

that person as well as the body corporate commits the offence and is liable to
be proceeded against and punished accordingly.

3.4

In  subsection (3)  “ director ”, in relation to a body corporate whose
affairs are managed by its members, means a member of the body corporate.

3.5

Subsection (3)  also applies to a body that is not a body corporate, with the
substitution for the reference to a director of the body of a reference—

3.5.a

in the case of a partnership, to a partner;

3.5.b

in the case of an unincorporated body other than a partnership—

3.5.b.i

where the body’s affairs are managed by its members, to a member of the body;

3.5.b.ii

in any other case, to a member of the governing body.

#### Enforcement powers of local weights and measures authorities etc

4

4.1

A local weights and measures authority in England and the Department for the
Economy in Northern Ireland may enforce the provisions of this Act.

4.2

For the investigatory powers available for the purpose of enforcing this Act,
see Schedule 5 to the Consumer Rights Act 2015.

4.3

In paragraph 10 of Schedule 5 to the Consumer Rights Act 2015 (duties and
powers to which Schedule 5 applies), at the appropriate place insert—

                                    section 4 (1)  of the Animals (Low-Welfare
Activities Abroad) Act 2023.

4.4

The Schedule  contains provision for the imposition of monetary penalties in
respect of offences under this Act.

### General

#### Regulations

5

5.1

The power to make activity regulations includes power to make—

5.1.a

different provision for different purposes or for different relevant parts of
the United Kingdom;

5.1.b

consequential, incidental, supplementary, transitional or saving provision.

5.2

Activity regulations made by the Secretary of State are to be made by
statutory instrument.

5.3

The power of the Department of Agriculture, Environment and Rural Affairs to
make activity regulations is exercisable by statutory rule for the purposes of
the Statutory Rules (Northern Ireland) Order 1979 ( S.I. 1979/1573 (N.I. 12)
).

5.4

The Secretary of State may not make a statutory instrument containing activity
regulations (whether alone or with other provision) unless a draft of the
instrument has been laid before, and approved by resolution of, each House of
Parliament.

5.5

The Department of Agriculture, Environment and Rural Affairs may not make
activity regulations unless a draft of the regulations has been laid before,
and approved by a resolution of, the Northern Ireland Assembly.

#### Interpretation

6

In this Act—

         “ activity regulations ” means regulations under section 1(1)(c);
“ animal ” means a vertebrate other than a human;
“ appropriate national authority ” means—
in relation to England, the Secretary of State;
in relation to Northern Ireland—
the Department of Agriculture, Environment and Rural Affairs, or
the Secretary of State acting with the consent of the Department;
“ appropriate national legislation ” means—
in relation to England, the Animal Welfare Act 2006;
in relation to Northern Ireland, the  Welfare of Animals Act (Northern
Ireland) 2011 (c. 16 (N.I.) );
references to “ offering ” to sell include making an invitation to treat;
“ relevant part of the United Kingdom ” means England or Northern Ireland;
“ vertebrate ” means any animal of the Sub-phylum Vertebrata of the Phylum
Chordata.

#### Extent, commencement and short title

7

7.1

This Act extends to England and Wales and Northern Ireland.

7.2

This Act comes into force at the end of the period of two months beginning
with the day on which it is passed.

7.3

This Act may be cited as the Animals (Low-Welfare Activities Abroad) Act 2023.

                 Monetary penalties

Section 4

##### Enforcement authority

1

In this Schedule, “ enforcement authority ” means—

1.a

a local weights and measures authority in England, or

1.b

the Department for the Economy in Northern Ireland.

##### Imposition of monetary penalties

2

2.1

Where an enforcement authority is satisfied beyond reasonable doubt that a
person has committed an offence under  section 1  or  2  in the authority’s
area, the authority may impose a monetary penalty on the person.

2.2

In this Schedule “ monetary penalty ” means a requirement to pay the
enforcement authority a penalty of an amount determined by the authority.

2.3

In Northern Ireland, the amount of the penalty may not exceed an amount
equivalent to level 5 on the standard scale.

##### Notices and representations

3

3.1

Where an enforcement authority proposes to impose a monetary penalty on a
person the authority must give the person a notice of what is proposed (an
“initial penalty notice”).

3.2

An initial penalty notice must offer the person the opportunity to avoid
liability in relation to a monetary penalty by payment of a sum specified in
the notice (which must be less than or equal to the amount of the proposed
penalty).

3.3

The person may make written representations and objections to the enforcement
authority in relation to the proposed imposition of the monetary penalty
within the period of 28 days beginning with the day on which the initial
penalty notice is given.

3.4

After the end of the period mentioned in  sub-paragraph (3)  the enforcement
authority must decide whether to give the person a notice imposing a monetary
penalty (a “final penalty notice”).

3.5

The enforcement authority may not give a person a final penalty notice if the
authority is no longer satisfied as mentioned in  paragraph 2 (1) .

3.6

The penalty specified in the final penalty notice may be—

3.6.a

a penalty of the amount proposed in the initial penalty notice, or

3.6.b

a penalty of a smaller amount.

3.7

An initial or final penalty notice may be withdrawn at any time by the
enforcement authority that issued it.

##### Information to be included in notices under 

4

4.1

An initial penalty notice must include information as to—

4.1.a

the grounds for the proposal to impose the monetary penalty,

4.1.b

the effect of payment of the sum mentioned in  paragraph 3 (2) ,

4.1.c

the period within which payment may be made so as to avoid liability for a
monetary penalty (which must be at least 28 days beginning with the day on
which the notice is given),

4.1.d

the right to make representations and objections within the period of 28 days
beginning with the day on which the notice is given, and

4.1.e

the effect of  paragraph 3 (5) .

4.2

A final penalty notice must include information as to—

4.2.a

the grounds for imposing the monetary penalty,

4.2.b

how payment may be made,

4.2.c

the period within which payment is to be made (which must be at least 28 days
beginning with the day on which the notice is given),

4.2.d

rights of appeal, and

4.2.e

the consequences of non-payment.

4.3

A final penalty notice may provide for interest or other penalties specified
in the notice to be payable in the event that payment is not made in the
period referred to in  sub-paragraph (2) (c) .

##### Monetary penalties: criminal proceedings

5

5.1

Where an initial penalty notice is given to a person (and has not been
withdrawn)—

5.1.a

no criminal proceedings for the relevant offence may be instituted against the
person in respect of the act or omission to which the notice relates before
the end of the period within which the person’s liability may be avoided as
mentioned in  paragraph 3 (2)  (see  paragraph 4 (1) (c) );

5.1.b

if the liability is so avoided the person may not at any time be convicted of
the relevant offence in relation to that act or omission.

5.2

Where a final penalty notice is given to a person (and has not been
withdrawn), the person may not at any time be convicted of the relevant
offence in respect of the act or omission to which the notice relates.

5.3

In this paragraph “ relevant offence ” means an offence under  section 1  or
2  in respect of which the monetary penalty is proposed to be imposed or is
imposed (as the case may be).

##### Appeals

6

6.1

A person to whom a final penalty notice is given under  paragraph 3  may
appeal against the decision to give the notice on any of the grounds mentioned
in  sub-paragraph (2) .

6.2

The grounds are—

6.2.a

that the decision was based on an error of fact;

6.2.b

that the decision was wrong in law;

6.2.c

that the decision was unreasonable;

6.2.d

that the amount of penalty imposed by the notice is unreasonable.

6.3

An appeal under this paragraph is—

6.3.a

in relation to a decision of a local weights and measures authority, to the
First-tier Tribunal;

6.3.b

in relation to a decision of the Department for the Economy in Northern
Ireland, to a county court.

6.4

On an appeal under this paragraph the tribunal or court may—

6.4.a

quash the final penalty notice,

6.4.b

confirm the final penalty notice, or

6.4.c

vary the final penalty notice by reducing the amount of the penalty.

6.5

Where an appeal under this paragraph is made—

6.5.a

the requirement to pay the monetary penalty is suspended pending the final
determination or withdrawal of the appeal, and

6.5.b

no interest or other penalty is payable by virtue of  paragraph 4 (3)  in
respect of the period during which that requirement is suspended.

##### Use of proceeds

7

7.1

Sums received by local weights and measures authorities pursuant to monetary
penalties imposed under this Schedule must be paid into the Consolidated Fund.

7.2

But before paying such sums into the Consolidated Fund a local weights and
measures authority may deduct—

7.2.a

the costs of investigating offences to which monetary penalties imposed by the
authority under this Schedule relate, and

7.2.b

the costs of imposing the penalties.

7.3

The Department for the Economy in Northern Ireland may, before paying sums
received by the Department pursuant to monetary penalties imposed under this
Schedule into the Consolidated Fund of Northern Ireland, deduct—

7.3.a

the costs of investigating offences to which monetary penalties imposed by the
Department under this Schedule relate, and

7.3.b

the costs of imposing the penalties.

##### Enforcement

8

If a person does not pay the whole or any part of a penalty which the person
is liable to pay under this Schedule (including an amount payable by virtue of
paragraph 4 (3) ), the penalty or part of the penalty is recoverable—

8.a

in the case of a penalty imposed by a local weights and measures authority, as
if it were payable under an order of the county court,

8.b

in the case of a penalty imposed by the Department for the Economy in Northern
Ireland, as if it were payable under an order of a county court in Northern
Ireland.
