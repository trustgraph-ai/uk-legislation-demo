## The Public Service Vehicles Accessibility (Amendment) Regulations (Northern Ireland) 2023

    Road Traffic And Vehicles

  Made   5th September 2023

  Coming into operation   1st October 2023

      The Department for Infrastructure , makes the following Regulations in
exercise of the powers conferred by sections 41(2) and 45(1)(b) of the
Disability Discrimination Act 1995  and now vested in it .

### Citation and commencement

1

These Regulations may be cited as the Public Service Vehicles Accessibility
(Amendment) Regulations (Northern Ireland) 2023 and shall come into operation
on 1st October 2023.

### Amendment of the Public Service Vehicles Accessibility Regulations

2

2.1

The Public Service Vehicles Accessibility Regulations (Northern Ireland) 2003
are amended in accordance with paragraph (2).

2.2

In regulation 7 (applications and fees for accessibility certificates), in
paragraph (2)—

2.2.a

in sub-paragraph (a)—

2.2.a.i

in head (i) for “£28.00” substitute  “£46.00” ; and

2.2.a.ii

in head (ii) for “£55.00” substitute  “£70.50” ; and

2.2.b

in sub-paragraph (b)—

2.2.b.i

in head (i) for “£10.00” substitute  “£17.00” ; and

2.2.b.ii

in head (ii) for “£20.00” substitute  “£38.00” .

## EXPLANATORY NOTE

    (This note is not part of the Regulations)

  These Regulations amend the Public Service Vehicles Accessibility
Regulations (Northern Ireland) 2003 in order to increase the fees payable in
respect of applications for accessibility certificates.

  On first application, where the vehicle is required to comply with the only
one of Schedules 1, 2 or 3 the fee is increased from £28.00 to £46.00 and on
subsequent application the fee is increased from £10.00 to £17.00.

  On first application, where the vehicle is required to comply with Schedules
1 and 2 or Schedules 1 and 3 the fee is increased from £55.00 to £70.50 and on
subsequent application the fee is increased from £20.00 to £38.00.

  An Explanatory Memorandum and Regulatory Impact Assessment have been
produced and are available from Safe and Accessible Travel Division,
Department for Infrastructure, Clarence Court, 10-18 Adelaide Street, Town
Parks, Belfast BT2 8GB or online alongside this Regulation at
