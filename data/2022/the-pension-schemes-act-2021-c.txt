## The Pension Schemes Act 2021 (Commencement No. 6) Order (Northern Ireland) 2022

    Pensions

  Made   28th October 2022

      The Department for Communities makes the following Order in exercise of
the powers conferred by section 131(2) and (4) of the Pension Schemes Act 2021
.

### Citation

1

This Order may be cited as the Pension Schemes Act 2021 (Commencement No. 6)
Order (Northern Ireland) 2022.

### Appointed day

2

The day appointed for the coming into operation of section 120 of, and
Schedule 9 to, the Pension Schemes Act 2021 (pensions dashboards: Northern
Ireland), in so far as they are not already in operation , is 31st October
2022.

      Sealed with the Official Seal of the Department for Communities on 28th
October 2022       (L.S.)   Anne McCleary   A senior officer of the Department
for Communities

## EXPLANATORY NOTE

    (This note is not part of the Order)

  This Order brings into operation section 120 of, and Schedule 9 to, the
Pension Schemes Act 2021, in so far as they are not already in operation, on
31st October 2022.

  Schedule 9 to the Pension Schemes Act 2021 (pensions dashboards: Northern
Ireland)—         inserts Articles 215A (qualifying pensions dashboard
service), 215B (qualifying pension dashboard service: further provision) and
215C (Articles 215A and 215B: interpretation) into the Pensions (Northern
Ireland) Order 2005 ( S.I. 2005/255 (N.I. 1) ) (“ the 2005 Order ”) which,
amongst other things, confer powers on the Department for Communities to make
regulations to support the provision of pensions information to individuals by
means of a pensions dashboard service;           inserts Articles 215D
(information from occupational pension schemes), 215E (information from
occupational pension schemes: further provision), 215F (Articles 215D and
215E: interpretation) and 215G (compliance) into the 2005 Order to make
further provision relating to the giving of pensions information to
individuals via certain pensions dashboard services and any pensions dashboard
service provided for or on behalf of the Money and Pensions Service;
adds further powers to the list in Part 1 of Schedule 1 to the 2005 Order
(delegation) of the Pensions Regulator’s powers in relation to which the
Department for Communities can make regulations.
