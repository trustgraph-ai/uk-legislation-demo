## Motor Vehicles (Compulsory Insurance) Act 2022

      B e it enacted  by the Queen’s most Excellent Majesty, by and with the
advice and consent of the Lords Spiritual and Temporal, and Commons, in this
present Parliament assembled, and by the authority of the same, as follows:—

### Retained EU law relating to compulsory insurance for motor vehicles

1

After section 156 of the Road Traffic Act 1988 insert—

####### Retained EU law relating to compulsory insurance

######## Retained EU law relating to compulsory insurance

1.156A

1.156A.1

To the extent that Article 3 of the 2009 Motor Insurance Directive (as it had
effect at any time) is relevant to any question as to the interpretation or
effect of any provision of this Part, references in that Article to liability
in respect of the use of vehicles are to be read as not including liability in
respect of the use in Great Britain of vehicles—

1.156A.1.a

other than motor vehicles, or

1.156A.1.b

otherwise than on a road or other public place.

1.156A.2

Subsection  (1)  does not apply in relation to any question for the purposes
of section 145(3)(aa) or (b) as to the interpretation or effect of the law on
compulsory insurance of, or applicable in, a member State or Northern Ireland.

1.156A.3

Relevant section 4 rights cease to be recognised and available so far as they
relate to compensation in connection with the use in Great Britain of
vehicles—

1.156A.3.a

other than motor vehicles, or

1.156A.3.b

otherwise than on a road or other public place.

1.156A.4

Accordingly, to the extent that it is inconsistent with subsection  (1)  or
(3) , retained case law ceases to have effect.

1.156A.5

In this section—

      “ the 2009 Motor Insurance Directive ” means  Directive 2009/103/EC  of
the European Parliament and of the Council of 16 September 2009 relating to
insurance against civil liability in respect of the use of motor vehicles, and
the enforcement of the obligation to insure against such liability;
“ relevant section 4 rights ” means section 4 rights which—         are
recognised and available in the law of England and Wales or the law of
Scotland, and           derive from the obligation imposed on the United
Kingdom by Article 10 of the 2009 Motor Insurance Directive as it had effect
immediately before IP completion day (which relates to compensation in
connection with the use of vehicles in cases where drivers are uninsured or
untraced);                 “ retained case law ” has the same meaning as in
the European Union (Withdrawal) Act 2018 (see section 6(7) of that Act);
“ section 4 rights ” means rights, powers, liabilities, obligations,
restrictions, remedies and procedures which continue to be recognised and
available in domestic law by virtue of section 4 of the European Union
(Withdrawal) Act 2018 (saving for rights etc under section 2(1) of the ECA),
including those rights, powers, liabilities, obligations, restrictions,
remedies and procedures—         as modified by domestic law from time to
time, and           as they apply to the Crown.

1.156A.6

Nothing in this section applies in relation to the use of a vehicle before the
day on which section  1  of the Motor Vehicles (Compulsory Insurance) Act 2022
comes into force.

### Commencement, extent and short title

2

2.1

This Act comes into force at the end of the period of two months beginning
with the day on which it is passed.

2.2

This Act extends to England and Wales and Scotland.

2.3

This Act may be cited as the Motor Vehicles (Compulsory Insurance) Act 2022.
